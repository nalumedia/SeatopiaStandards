Methodology & Sources

Seatopia data: Independent third-party lab averages per 100g edible portion. Sources include Edacious (CLIA-certified, full fatty acid panel) and Light Labs (ISO 17025). Multiple test lots where available. Raw, uncooked basis unless otherwise noted.

Edacious Comps: Competitor products (Mowi, Walmart Chile, Costco Coho, Wild Alaskan Co., McFarland Springs, Riverance, Kilic, Amazon Pink) tested at the same Edacious lab using the same full-panel fatty-acid assay as Seatopia's own QC program. This is the cleanest available apples-to-apples comparison — no lab-to-lab methodology drift. Originally reported per 113g; converted to per 100g for consistency. These are NOT Seatopia products.

OmegaQuant reference: Adapted from Harris et al., Current Atherosclerosis Reports 2008;10:503-509. Values based on USDA Nutrient Data Lab and are for fish cooked with dry heat unless otherwise noted. Originally reported per 3 oz (85g), converted to per 100g by multiplying by 100/85. Farmed Atlantic Salmon ranges reflect variable EPA+DHA based on fish feed (Sprague et al., Scientific Reports 2016;6:21892).

Supplement data: Per capsule or per teaspoon. Not directly comparable to per-100g fish values — shown for context only. A standard fish oil capsule contains ~300 mg EPA+DHA.

Mercury (Hg, ppm): Light Labs heavy-metals panel (ICP-MS), reported in ppb and converted to ppm (÷1000). LOQ 10 ppb (0.010 ppm); results reported as "Below LOQ" are shown as <0.010 ppm. Benchmarks: FDA action level for methylmercury in commercial seafood = 1.0 ppm; EPA/FDA "Best Choices" (recommended for pregnancy and young children) = <0.15 ppm; "Good Choices" = <0.23 ppm. Every Seatopia product tested falls within the Best Choices tier — the highest single value is Esteros Lubimar Branzino at 0.100 ppm (10% of FDA limit, 67% of Best Choices ceiling). Average across the 20 tested Seatopia products is ~0.025 ppm.

Microplastics (MP): EMSL Analytical 6-polymer panel via Light Labs — HDPE, LDPE, PET, PP, PS, PVC — using FTIR / optical microscopy with a Limit of Detection of 1 microparticle per polymer. The chip "0/6" means zero of six target polymers were detected against that 1-microparticle floor (not "below an unknown threshold"). 15 Seatopia products have been screened; remaining products pending. No microparticles have been detected in any Seatopia sample tested to date.

Important caveats: OmegaQuant values are for cooked fish (dry heat); Seatopia lab data is for raw product. Cooking can concentrate omega-3 by ~15-20% due to moisture loss, meaning raw-basis Seatopia values may slightly understate cooked equivalents. Mercury and microplastics testing is per-lot — values shown reflect the most recent COA available for each farm and may vary between lots. Species, farming practices, seasonality, and diet all affect omega-3 content. This comparison is directional, not absolute.
